Voice Assistants (Siri, Alexa)

Voice assistants such as Siri, Alexa, and Google Assistant are examples of artificial intelligence used in everyday life which use AI to recognise human speech, understand questions, and provide spoken responses.

These systems rely on technologies such as speech recognition and natural language processing to work. For example, when a user asks a question or gives a command, the voice assistant analyses the input, processes the request, and then produces an appropriate response.

Voice assistants are used to set reminders, play music, check weather/time, control smart home devices, and search for information. This makes everyday tasks quicker and more convenient for users.

Recommendation Systems (Netflix, YouTube)

Platforms such as Netflix, YouTube, Spotify,and online shopping websites make use of artificial intelligence to suggest content or products to users.

These systems use AI to analyse user behaviour, such as what videos are watched, what products are viewed, or what music is listened to. Based on this data, the system predicts what the user might like and makes personalised recommendations which help users discover new content more easily and improve their overall experience on a platform.

Chatbots

Chatbot is an example artificial intelligence that people interact with in everyday life. They are mostly used on websites, mobile apps, and social media platforms to provide customer support and answer questions.

Chatbots use AI to understand user input and respond in a human-like way. Some chatbots are rule-based, while more advanced ones use machine learning to improve their responses based on previous conversations.

Chatbots are useful because they can provide quick instant responses, operate 24/7, and reduce the need for human customer service staff. For example, many companies use chatbots to help users track orders, book appointments, or get quick answers to common questions.

Smart Home Devices

Smart home devices are everyday technologies that use artificial intelligence to make homes more convenient and efficient. These devices include smart speakers, smart lights, smart thermostats, and security cameras.

AI allows smart home devices to learn user preferences and automate tasks. For example, a smart thermostat can learn when a user is usually at home and adjust the temperature automatically, helping to save energy.

Smart home devices can be controlled using voice assistants or mobile apps, making them easy to use. They improve comfort, safety, and energy efficiency, which is why they are becoming more popular in modern homes.

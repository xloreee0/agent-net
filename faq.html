Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Artificial Intelligence.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the ability of a computer or machine to perform tasks that would normally require human intelligence. This includes things like understanding speech, recognising images, making decisions, and learning from experience.

Voice assistants use a combination of technologies to understand and respond to speech:

  • Speech recognition converts spoken words into text
  • Natural language processing (NLP) understands the meaning of the text
  • A response is generated and spoken back to the user

Machine learning is a type of Artificial Intelligence where a computer learns from data without being explicitly programmed. Instead of following fixed rules, the system identifies patterns in large amounts of data and improves its performance over time through experience.

Netflix uses an AI-powered recommendation system that analyses your viewing behaviour. It looks at factors such as:

  • What you have watched and rated highly
  • How long you watched something before stopping
  • What similar users have enjoyed
  • The time of day you tend to watch

Based on this data, it predicts content you are likely to enjoy.

AI itself is a tool — whether it is beneficial or harmful depends on how it is used. Current AI systems are designed for specific tasks and are not capable of independent thought or decision-making outside of their purpose. Researchers and organisations around the world are working on guidelines to ensure AI is developed safely and responsibly.

A chatbot is a specific application of Artificial Intelligence. AI is the broader technology that powers many systems, while a chatbot is a program designed to simulate conversation with users. Not all chatbots use advanced AI — some follow simple pre-written rules, while others use machine learning to improve their responses.

AI is already automating some repetitive tasks, which has changed certain job roles. However, AI is also creating new types of jobs in technology, data science, and AI development. Most experts believe AI will change the nature of work rather than replace humans entirely, particularly in roles requiring creativity, empathy, and complex decision-making.

Smart home devices use AI to learn user preferences and automate tasks. For example:

  • Smart thermostats learn your schedule and adjust temperature automatically
  • Smart speakers use voice recognition to respond to commands
  • Security cameras use AI to detect unusual activity
  • Smart lights can adjust brightness based on time of day
